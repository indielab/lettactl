# Seasonal Ingredient Guide

## Spring (March - May)
### Vegetables
- Asparagus, artichokes, peas, spring onions
- Baby lettuce, spinach, arugula
- Radishes, carrots, new potatoes
- Fresh herbs: chives, dill, parsley

### Fruits  
- Strawberries, rhubarb
- Early citrus: lemons, limes
- Apricots (late spring)

### Best Uses
- Light salads with fresh greens
- Grilled asparagus and vegetables
- Fresh herb sauces and pestos
- Strawberry desserts and jams

## Summer (June - August)
### Vegetables
- Tomatoes, zucchini, eggplant, peppers
- Corn, green beans, okra
- Cucumbers, summer squash
- Fresh basil, oregano, thyme

### Fruits
- Stone fruits: peaches, plums, nectarines, cherries
- Berries: blueberries, blackberries, raspberries
- Melons: watermelon, cantaloupe, honeydew

### Best Uses
- Fresh salsas and gazpacho
- Grilled vegetables and fruits
- Berry cobblers and pies
- Cold soups and salads

## Fall (September - November)
### Vegetables
- Winter squash: butternut, acorn, delicata
- Root vegetables: sweet potatoes, carrots, beets
- Brussels sprouts, cauliflower, cabbage
- Apples, cranberries, pears

### Best Uses
- Roasted root vegetables
- Hearty soups and stews
- Apple and pear desserts
- Warm spice blends: cinnamon, nutmeg, cloves

## Winter (December - February)
### Vegetables
- Hearty greens: kale, collards, Swiss chard
- Storage vegetables: potatoes, onions, garlic
- Citrus: oranges, grapefruits, lemons
- Winter herbs: rosemary, sage, thyme

### Best Uses
- Braised dishes and slow-cooked meals
- Citrus-based sauces and dressings
- Comfort foods and warming spices
- Preserved and pickled vegetables
